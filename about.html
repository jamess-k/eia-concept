Our Program's History

English in Action is a relatively new program at Cornell University in particular, however we have existed as a national organization since 1994, when we were initially created at the Roaring Fork Valley in Colorado by the Basalt Regional Library to help out a large influx of immigrant workers who did not speak English. Back then, we were called the Adult Literacy Program and only operated on a small scale of a few dozen members at the time, each focused on one on one tutoring groups to help bridge the differences in language and culture between members of the community.

In 2005 the modern day English in Action was born due to a necessity to move out of the small library as we grew in size. Founder Julie Fox-Rubin took the new program under her wing and spread it further beyond a single brick and mortar building to eventually become the large entity it is today. Since 2008, English in Action has operated as an independent nonprofit organization when our current director, Lara Beaulieu, was onboarded. In the modern day, we are headquarted in El Jebel however our online presence extends across the country where we now found our presence at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York.
